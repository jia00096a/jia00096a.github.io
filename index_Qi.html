Qi Jia

Developing energy-efficient memory technologies through voltage-controlled magnetism, spintronics, and device physics.

I am a Ph.D. candidate in Electrical Engineering at the University of Minnesota, Twin Cities, advised by Prof. Jian-Ping Wang. My research focuses on spintronic memory devices, voltage-controlled magnetic switching, stochastic magnetic tunnel junctions, spin-torque measurements, and emerging quantum materials for next-generation memory and computing hardware.

I combine device fabrication, electrical and RF characterization, statistical data analysis, and physics-based modeling to understand how material structure, interfacial effects, and device geometry control memory-device performance, variability, and reliability.

Research Areas

My work connects magnetic-device physics with semiconductor memory technology, with an emphasis on low-power switching, stochastic operation, and experimentally grounded device modeling.

  • Voltage-controlled exchange coupling
  • Magnetic tunnel junction devices
  • RF and spin-torque characterization
  • Reliability, variability, and statistical analysis
  • Altermagnetic RuO2 and spin transport
  • Memory-device physics and characterization

Featured Research

Selected projects organized as research stories rather than only publication entries.

Voltage-Controlled Exchange Coupling in MTJs

Demonstrated electric-field control of interlayer exchange coupling in perpendicular magnetic tunnel junctions, enabling ultra-low-power stochastic signal manipulation and voltage-assisted magnetic switching.

  • Related Papers

Strain-Induced Altermagnetic Spin Splitting in RuO2

Investigated how epitaxial strain stabilizes symmetry-dependent spin-splitting torques in RuO2 thin films, linking transport signatures with magnetic characterization and first-principles calculations.

  • Related Papers

Hardware Randomness from Magnetic Devices

Developed perpendicular magnetic tunnel junction platforms for robust high-entropy random number generation across a wide operating range, combining device physics, statistical testing, and reliability analysis.
